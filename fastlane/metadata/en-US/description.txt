Echo: Audiobook Study Player

For Every Mind.

Most audiobook apps are built for passive listening. Echo is built for learning, retention, and brains that need a little extra flexibility. Whether you want flashcards with the narrator's voice on them, a synced ePub or PDF that scrolls with the audio, or a custom Apple Watch remote you design yourself, Echo turns your DRM-free audiobooks into a true study tool.

THE ORIGIN STORY

The idea for Echo started behind the wheel of a mail delivery vehicle. Jumping in and out of a car thirty times a day makes it impossible to focus on complex non-fiction. Traditional apps constantly lost the playback spot, watch controls were too small to tap quickly, and retaining information with intermittent attention was a constant struggle.

Echo was built to fix all of that. Designed originally to accommodate AuDHD processing, its robust toolset is perfect for lifelong learners, commuters, and anyone who actually wants to absorb their books.

FEATURES BUILT FOR FOCUS

• Smart Rewind — Never lose context when you hit play after a pause. The longer you've been away, the further it rewinds. Interruptions stop costing you anything.

• Chapter Looping — Put a single chapter on repeat until the concepts are fully absorbed, or loop the exact passage between two bookmarks for targeted review.

• Voice Memo Bookmarks — Hold a button and speak your thought. Memos can play back inline when the narration reaches them — your past self briefing your present self.

• Photo Bookmarks — Attach a photo of where you are; the player artwork switches to your photo as you pass that moment again. Your brain files where you were alongside what you heard — Echo turns that into a memory tool.

• Spaced Repetition (SRS) — A built-in Anki-style flashcard system (SM-2). Make cards from bookmarks or passages, attach the audio clip, and review daily — on your phone or hands-free on Apple Watch, with stats and an optional reminder. It even imports Anki-style decks.

• Synced ePub Reader — Read along while you listen. The text scrolls with the narration, tap any paragraph to jump the audio there, search the full book and leap to the spoken moment, and see the diagrams exactly when they're mentioned.

• On-Device Auto-Alignment — Echo's speech recognition (WhisperKit + CoreML) aligns text to audio in tiers: chapter snap, drift detection, and word-level repair. Entirely on your device — your audio never goes to any cloud.

• PDF Companions — Align PDF pages to the audio with a precision scrubber joystick, and bookmark pages with their exact zoom and position.

• Pristine Speed Control — Listen at 1.25x (or faster) with zero pitch distortion. Every book remembers its own speed.

• Apple Watch Remote — Up to 25 customizable buttons across 5 pages, designed by you, from your phone. Digital Crown scrubbing or volume, hands-free flashcard review, voice memo bookmarks, and a Pomodoro focus timer — on your wrist.

ACCESSIBILITY & NEURODIVERSITY FIRST

Echo was designed from the ground up for neurodivergent learning styles. The infinity icon (∞) in silver and gold is a nod to the AuDHD community, and the name reflects how many of these brains work: text and audio echoing back and forth, reinforcing each other.

• Dyslexia-Optimized Fonts — Choose between research-backed Lexend (improved reading fluency) or OpenDyslexic (weighted letterforms to combat reversal).

• Hybrid Reading + Listening — Never forced to learn by audio alone. The synced reader gives you text as an anchor while you listen.

PRIVATE BY DESIGN

No accounts, no analytics, no tracking, no servers. Your books, bookmarks, photos, and flashcards stay on your device. Echo is open source (MIT) — every line is public.
